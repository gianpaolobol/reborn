# 09 — Go-To-Market

## Initial wedge

The first wedge should be narrow enough to work and broad enough to prove the platform:

- broken or missing plastic components;
- objects where spare parts are hard to find;
- parts suitable for 3D printing;
- maker/provider community able to supply solutions;
- users willing to upload photos and measurements.

Suggested early categories:

- household appliance accessories;
- furniture connectors;
- bicycle accessories;
- hobby/equipment parts;
- small plastic mechanisms;
- discontinued or obsolete components.

---

## Supply acquisition

### Makers

Value proposition:

- earn from useful repair models;
- receive bounties;
- build verified reputation;
- see real-world impact;
- create a portfolio around repair, not generic downloads.

### Providers

Value proposition:

- receive qualified local jobs;
- reduce customer acquisition cost;
- differentiate by quality;
- access enterprise repair demand;
- monetize idle printer capacity.

---

## Demand acquisition

Channels:

- SEO around repair queries;
- local repair communities;
- 3D printing groups;
- maker fairs;
- sustainability events;
- repair cafés;
- social videos showing before/after repair;
- partnerships with shops and labs;
- enterprise circular economy pilots.

---

## Marketplace liquidity strategy

Avoid launching as a generic empty marketplace.

Start with guided repair intake and manually/semiautomatically route early cases.

Liquidity sequence:

1. collect repair cases;
2. identify repeated categories;
3. recruit makers for those categories;
4. recruit providers in matching geographies;
5. verify outcomes;
6. turn repeated cases into indexed repair paths.

---

## Early credibility assets

- before/after repair stories;
- verified model case studies;
- provider quality examples;
- sustainability metrics;
- maker earnings examples;
- enterprise pilot reports.
