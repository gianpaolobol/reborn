# 11 — Risks and Assumptions

## Product assumptions

1. Users will upload enough information to identify objects/components.
2. Many repair cases can be solved through existing CAD, compatible parts or local production.
3. Makers are willing to create repair-specific models if monetization and reputation are clear.
4. Providers are willing to accept platform commission in exchange for qualified demand.
5. Outcome feedback can be collected consistently enough to improve trust.

---

## Technical risks

- AI recognition may be uncertain for obscure components.
- AI-generated models may not be dimensionally usable without human validation.
- Fit and safety requirements can vary by category.
- Knowledge Graph structure may become complex too early.
- Provider order workflow can require operational support.

---

## Business risks

- marketplace cold start;
- low user willingness to pay for small repairs;
- provider quality inconsistency;
- legal issues around branded components;
- safety/liability for functional parts;
- IP conflicts on replacement models;
- competition from established model platforms.

---

## Mitigation principles

1. Start narrow.
2. Verify outcomes.
3. Separate AI suggestions from verified models.
4. Use disclaimers and category restrictions for safety-critical parts.
5. Reward high-quality makers/providers.
6. Build data advantage through completed repair cases.
